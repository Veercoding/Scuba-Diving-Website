HOW TO BECOME AN ADVANCED OPEN WATER DIVER

INDEPENDENT STUDY

eLearning

Interactive lessons on 13 well-liked speciality dives are included in the Advanced Open Water Diver eLearning course, including altitude, boat, digital underwater imaging, drift, dry suit, fish ID, night, buoyancy, search and recovery, underwater naturalist, and wreck diving. You'll study deep and navigation diving in addition to three chosen specializations.



  • eLearning time commitment: 6-8 hours


AT THE DIVE SHOP

With Your Instructor

Five dives are included in the course: a navigation dive, three speciality dives, and a deep dive below 18 meters/60 feet. Learn to dive, develop your confidence, and become better at it.



  • Prerequisites: Junior Open Water Diver or Open Water Diver qualification, or an equivalent

  • Time commitment: 2 to 3 days.

  • Minimum age is twelve years old.

  • Depth:
    The maximum depth is up to 30 meters/100 feet, depending on your age.

HOW TO TAKE GREAT UNDERWATER PHOTOS

INDEPENDENT STUDY

eLearning

Learning the basics of underwater photography is simple with PADI eLearning. Study composition, lighting, shooting angles, and proper photography etiquette. Utilizing a computer or mobile device, you can study at your own pace both online and off. For in-water instruction or any other time you have a question, get in touch with your instructor.



  • eLearning time commitment: 2-4 hours


AT THE DIVE SHOP

With Your Instructor

You will gain practical experience using a compact camera or action camera (use your own or inquire about rentals) during two dives. To capture outstanding photographs, you'll employ the SEA (Shoot, Examine, Adjust) strategy.



  • Prerequisites: Certified diver / Jr. OW Diver (or qualifying certification)

  • Time commitment: 2 days.

  • Minimum age is 10 years or older.

HOW TO START DEEP DIVING

When you dive below 18 metres/60 feet, there are special safety considerations. If you're ready to expand your boundaries, a PADI Instructor will help you gain the skills and confidence to explore to a maximum depth of 40 metres/130 feet.

INDEPENDENT STUDY

eLearning

PADI eLearning makes it easy to fit scuba lessons into a busy schedule. Complete the independent study part of your course whenever, wherever it's convenient for you. Work at your own pace, online or offline, using a computer or mobile device. Connect with your instructor for inwater training, or whenever you have a question.



  • eLearning time commitment: 2-4 hours


AT THE DIVE SHOP

With Your Instructor

During four dives with a PADI Instructor, you'll practice descents, buoyancy and safety stops. Through hands-on exercises, you'll discover how deep dives affect color, physical objects and your brain.



  • Prerequisites: PADI Adventure Diver (or qualifying certification)

  • Time commitment: 2-3 days.

  • Minimum age is 15 years or older.

  • Total time commitment: 2-3 days (dives must be completed over a minimum of two days)



LEARN HOW TO NAVIGATE UNDERWATER.

Underwater, it's simple to become lost if you don't know how to use a compass or the environment's natural cues. Avoid long, strenuous swims by learning how to navigate back to the boat or land without coming back to the surface..

INDEPENDENT STUDY

eLearning

It's simple to develop your underwater navigation skills with PADI eLearning. You can begin your course whenever and wherever is most convenient for you. Using a computer or mobile device, work at your own speed both online and offline. For in-water instruction or any other time you have a question, get in touch with your instructor.

  • eLearning time commitment: 2-4 hours


AT THE DIVE SHOP

With Your Instructor

On the surface, practice using a compass. Then, throughout three dives, put your knowledge to use. Learn distance estimation techniques, pay attention to the environment for cues, and practice returning to the boat or land.




  • Prerequisites: PADI (Junior) Open Water Diver (or qualifying entry-level certification)

  • Time commitment: 2-3 days.

  • Minimum age is 10 years or older.



HOW TO START DRYSUIT DIVING.

You will get the knowledge and skills necessary to dive in a drysuit with ease and assurance during one pool session and two open water dives. You can adjust your buoyancy and accelerate your learning curve with the aid of an experienced instructor.

INDEPENDENT STUDY

eLearning

Scuba classes are simple to fit into a hectic schedule thanks to PADI eLearning. When and whenever it is most convenient for you, finish the independent study component of your course. Using a computer or mobile device, work at your own speed both online and offline. For in-water instruction or any other time you have a question, get in touch with your instructor.

  • eLearning time commitment: 2-4 hours


AT THE DIVE SHOP

With Your Instructor

Firstly you'll practice taking your drysuit on and off, then you will determine how much weight you need, then later on you will try to master the basic drysuit skills in a pool before making two open water dives




  • Prerequisites: PADI (Junior) Open Water Diver (or qualifying entry-level certification)

  • Time commitment: 2-3 days.

  • Minimum age is 10 years or older.
